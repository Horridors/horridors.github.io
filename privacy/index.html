Horridors

Privacy Policy

Effective: April 18, 2026  ·  Last updated: April 18, 2026

Horridors (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a single-player offline game designed by 7-year-old Chester Edmunds and published by his father, Dan Edmunds. This policy explains our data practices in plain English.

Short version: we don’t collect any data about you, at all. The game runs entirely on your phone.

Information We Collect

None. Horridors runs entirely on your device. Specifically, the app:

  • Does not create an account
  • Does not request your name, email, phone number, or any personal details
  • Does not track your location
  • Does not access your contacts, camera, microphone, or photos
  • Does not include analytics, advertising SDKs, or third‑party trackers
  • Does not send any data over the internet

Game Save Data

Horridors does not save any progress or settings to the cloud. Any local save data stays on your device and is deleted when you uninstall the app.

Permissions

The app requests only:

  • Internet — required by the Android system for the embedded web view component. The app itself does not make any network requests.

Children’s Privacy

Horridors is designed for players aged 7 and up and is safe for children. Because we collect no data at all, we comply with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and GDPR‑K requirements by default.

Third‑Party Services

Horridors does not use any third‑party services, SDKs, analytics platforms, advertising networks, or crash reporting tools.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever add features that collect data, this policy will be updated before such features are released, and you will be notified in the app and on the Play Store listing.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Please contact Dan Edmunds — publisher of Horridors (on behalf of Chester Edmunds, the game’s creator).

This app was made by a kid who just wanted to build a fun game. Your privacy matters and we don’t want it.
